## The first ninety days

The first quarter should create decision-grade evidence and shared
infrastructure, not a large platform commitment.

```{=latex}
\ExecutiveDecisionStrip
  {Produce a day-90 gate decision, not a technology launch.}
  {Owner, task baseline, mandate, evaluation, lawful route, shadow record, and signers.}
  {Calendar progress overrides an unresolved material risk or missing owner.}
```

The plan is built as six fifteen-day evidence gates. Each stage produces a
decision artifact and names the condition that prevents progression.

## The board dashboard

Keep it decision-oriented. Each card shows one primary signal, the evidence
needed to interpret it, and the threshold that changes the board decision.

```{=latex}
\ExecutiveDecisionStrip
  {Read one primary operating signal per card.}
  {Baseline, current result, target or stop threshold, owner, and source.}
  {Do not close a review without a named continue, tune, hold, demote, stop, or exit action.}
```

## The 12–24 month watch

Maintain one row per signal: capability, cost, sovereignty, trader-side
agents, standards, and law. Each row records probability, earliest plausible
decision date, official source, no-regret preparation, irreversible
commitment to avoid, readiness, owner, and next review. These are the complete
minimum fields for this brief. Do not buy against a forecast; build options
that remain useful if it is wrong.

## Five operating rules

```{=latex}
\ExecutiveRuleStrip{01}{Govern one real task}{Give it a baseline, accountable owner, and an accurate pipeline-or-agent description.}
\ExecutiveRuleStrip{02}{Record both dials}{Limit autonomy one rung at a time and agency to the reach actually needed.}
\ExecutiveRuleStrip{03}{Keep sovereignty and evidence}{The administration owns the approved path, evaluation, and exportable evidence room.}
\ExecutiveRuleStrip{04}{Earn the right to operate}{Carry a mandate, assurance record, viable human route, demotion trigger, and tested fallback.}
\ExecutiveRuleStrip{05}{Treat change and renewal as decisions}{Material changes re-enter the gate; renewal may continue, compete, migrate, demote, or retire.}
```

> **THE FINAL BOARD QUESTION.** The mandate of customs remains collect
> revenue, protect the border, and facilitate trade. Agentic AI changes the
> operating model, not institutional responsibility. Per task, ask: **what
> may it decide; what can it technically do; who signed; what evidence earned
> that authority; and when will we take it back?** A leadership team that can
> answer is running an AI programme, not following a vendor narrative.
