## The first ninety days

The first quarter should create decision-grade evidence and shared
infrastructure, not a large platform commitment.

**Days 1–15 — establish command.** Name the executive owner and a small
cross-functional gate team: operations, legal/privacy, security, data/IT,
procurement, workforce, and finance. Inventory live and experimental AI,
including personal subscriptions and API keys. Stop new sensitive data
from entering unapproved paths and provide a sanctioned synthetic/public-
data sandbox. Select one bounded case loop and measure its current volume,
time, quality, exceptions, cost, and owner. **Stop and escalate:** without a
named owner and bounded loop by day 15, the executive sponsor resolves the
mandate or pauses selection; no vendor meeting becomes a pilot.

**Days 16–30 — define the task.** Complete the decision card, Autonomy
Ladder rung, agency profile, data-flow/residency map, applicability record,
affected-party route, initial value bridge, and demotion/fallback design.
Choose the approved corpus and feeds. Name their custodians and freshness
rules. **Stop and escalate:** an open sovereignty, legal, or mandate gap
holds Gates 1–4; the named legal, security, or data owner closes it, or the
team returns to the sandbox and a lower-authority task.

**Days 31–45 — prepare evidence.** Build a representative evaluation set
with hard, multilingual, incomplete, duplicate, and hostile cases. Keep a
sealed holdout. Define baseline, repeated trials, error taxonomy,
abstention, latency, cost, security, human-queue, and complete-case
thresholds before testing. **Stop and escalate:** without a baseline,
holdout, and pre-committed thresholds, do not run a performance trial;
escalate the missing corpus or data-quality decision to its custodian.

**Days 46–60 — buy or build visibly.** Run the twenty-case pre-qualification
where a vendor is involved. Require the six-question response, lawful
deployment, component and subprocessor register, identity/tool diagram,
trajectory export, change matrix, incident cooperation, commercial
normalisation, knowledge transfer, and exit rehearsal. Start the evidence
room. **Stop and escalate:** if lawful deployment, trajectory export, or
exit rights cannot be evidenced, pause procurement and seek an approved
route, a different supplier, or a non-agentic alternative.

**Days 61–75 — run shadow mode.** Compare with the current workflow. Sample
trajectories. Measure correction and override reasons, state-dependent
failure, unit economics, exception capacity, and adoption. Run hostile-
document, excessive-authority, stale/conflicting-source, and
freeze-preserve-fallback exercises. **Stop and escalate:** a failed quality,
security, mandate, or human-capacity threshold ends shadow progression;
remediate, narrow authority, or retire the task before any live use.

**Days 76–90 — make a gate decision.** Publish a short memo: evidence,
threshold result, residual risk, control gaps, value range, legal/security
status, human capacity, and recommendation. The options are promote to a
limited live pilot, hold and tune, demote/simplify, or stop. A stopped pilot
that prevents an unsuitable production system is a successful governance
outcome. **Stop and escalate:** unresolved material risk, an overdue legal
review, or a missing accountable signer means hold, demote, or stop — never
promotion because the calendar says day 90.

## The board dashboard

Keep it decision-oriented:

| Question | Board metric and decision threshold |
|---|---|
| Is value being captured? | **Unit cost per completed case** — baseline, pilot actual, target, and volume; explain the named action converting capacity or quality into value. |
| Is quality acceptable? | **Precision, recall, and officer override rate by approved stratum**; each meets its pre-committed floor/ceiling, with abstentions and adverse burden shown separately. |
| Is authority contained? | **Mandate-conformance rate** — 100% of sampled trajectories inside the signed task, tools, data, and write scope; any unapproved reach is an immediate stop and incident. |
| Is the human route viable? | **95th-percentile exception-queue age** against service target, plus supervisor utilisation against the approved capacity ceiling. |
| Is the service lawful and sovereign? | **Applicability record status** — current, named reviewer, approved data path, and next legal-review date; no unowned material legal finding. |
| Can we investigate and exit? | **Evidence-room export test within the last 90 days**, with a successful trajectory export, fallback rehearsal, and owner for any failed exit dependency. |

## The 12–24 month watch

Maintain one row per signal: capability, cost, sovereignty, trader-side
agents, standards, and law. Each row records probability, earliest plausible
decision date, official source, no-regret preparation, irreversible
commitment to avoid, readiness, owner, and next review. These are the complete
minimum fields for this brief. Do not buy against a forecast; build options
that remain useful if it is wrong.

## Five operating rules

1. **Govern one real task.** Give it a baseline, accountable owner, and an
   accurate description of whether it is a fixed pipeline or adaptive agent.
2. **Record both dials.** Limit autonomy one rung at a time and agency to
   the reach actually needed; accountability remains with the administration.
3. **Keep sovereignty and evidence under administrative control.** No
   sensitive work enters consumer subscriptions or unreviewed paths, and the
   administration owns evaluation and an exportable evidence room.
4. **Earn the right to operate.** Every consequential task carries a
   mandate, operational assurance record, viable human route, demotion
   trigger, and tested fallback; twenty cases pre-qualify, not prove scale.
5. **Treat change and renewal as new decisions.** Material model, prompt,
   corpus, tool, identity, region, or dependency changes re-enter the gate;
   renewal may mean continue, compete, migrate, demote, or retire.

The mandate of customs remains collect revenue, protect the border, and
facilitate trade. Agentic AI changes the operating model, not institutional
responsibility. Per task, ask: **what may it decide; what can it technically
do; who signed; what evidence earned that authority; and when will we take
it back?** If the leadership team can answer, it is running an AI programme,
not following a technology category or vendor narrative.
